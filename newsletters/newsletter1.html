 ANS Student Conference: 
 Summary Deadline is Friday, Jan. 18! 

When: April 4-6
Where: Virginia Commonwealth University

In order to receive funding and logistics from NPRE and ANS respectively, you must be presenting research at the conference. Without research, you would have to pay your own way to the conference. If you're an ANS member and are hoping to attend and present research, we'll try to match you with NPRE research that would otherwise not be presented at the conference. If you're interested, either as an individual or a group, then check out a list of NPRE faculty research. Anybody can receive funding as long as they have something to present, and we want you all to come!

What you need to do this week:
  1. Submit your summary here. Be sure to follow the Call for Papers and Template guidelines! Note: you may need to become an ANS National member to submit. 
  2. Ask for help. If you are turned around in the submission process or need some guidance on how to write a summary, send a slack message to an executive board member. We are glad to help. It may help to look at some example abstracts and summaries. 

What you need to go:
  1. Become an ANS National Member (costs $35)
  2. Pay Virginia Commonwealth University for conference registration (costs ~$40)
  3. Become a dues paying member with ANS at UIUC (costs $10/semester ... if you have not paid)
  4. Communicate your conference involvement to ANS at UIUC by filling out this form.
  5. Join the #student_conf_2019 Slack channel.
  6. Have YOUR name on an accepted summary. 

Additional information and details about tours can be found at the 2019 Student Conference website.

 Luncheon with Dr. Ge Yang 
January 29
12:00 - 1:30 pm
Location TBD
Dr. Ge Yang, an Assistant Professor in the Department of Nuclear Energy at North Carolina State University, will be holding a luncheon with NPRE students on Tuesday, January 29. Pizza and drinks will be provided.

Bio: Dr. Yang received his B.S. (2001), M.S. (2004) and Ph.D. (2007) in materials science and engineering from Northwestern Polytechnical University. Prior to joining the NC State faculty, he spent ten years at U.S. Department of Energy’s Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL), working in Radiation Detectors and Nonproliferation R&D Group. During the period, he has been promoted through the ranks (Research Associate, Assistant Scientist, Associate Scientist and Scientist) because of his outstanding performance. Dr. Yang’s research has yielded 7 patents, 133 publications in top-ranked scientific journals and conference proceedings, and numerous invited presentations at various professional conferences. He is a two-time recipient of the prestigious R&D 100 Award, in 2009 and 2014, together with his collaborators for developing various compact sensors to detect and image radiation. He is also the co-Finalist of 2017 R&D 100 Award for developing novel electrode material for radiation detectors.
